Channels are the foundation of organization in Mattermost. They provide dedicated spaces for teams to communicate about specific topics, projects, or initiatives.

## Channel Types

Mattermost supports four distinct channel types, each serving different communication needs:

### Public Channels

**Identifier**: `O` (Open)

Public channels are visible to all team members and can be joined without an invitation:
- Discoverable via channel browser
- Searchable by all team members
- Ideal for company-wide announcements, general discussion, and team collaboration
- Maximum channels per team is configurable (default varies by installation)

**Default Channels**:
- **Town Square** - Default channel for all team members, perfect for general announcements
- **Off-Topic** - Casual conversations and non-work discussions

<Note>
Administrators can configure custom default channels that all users automatically join when added to a team.
</Note>

### Private Channels

**Identifier**: `P` (Private)

Private channels restrict access to invited members only:
- Not visible in channel browser to non-members
- Require invitation or admin addition to join
- Perfect for confidential projects, sensitive discussions, or focused teams
- Can be converted to public channels (with proper permissions)
- Support group constraints for synchronized membership

```go
// Channel type constants from source
const (
    ChannelTypeOpen    ChannelType = "O"  // Public
    ChannelTypePrivate ChannelType = "P"  // Private
    ChannelTypeDirect  ChannelType = "D"  // Direct Message
    ChannelTypeGroup   ChannelType = "G"  // Group Message
)
```

### Direct Messages (DMs)

**Identifier**: `D` (Direct)

One-on-one conversations between two users:
- Automatically created when messaging another user
- Private to the two participants
- Cannot be converted to other channel types
- Support all messaging features (files, reactions, threads)
- Cross-team - can DM any user in your organization

### Group Messages (GMs)

**Identifier**: `G` (Group)

Private conversations with multiple participants:
- Support 3-8 participants
- Cannot be discovered or joined - must be created or invited
- Automatic naming based on participant usernames
- Can be converted to private channels for enhanced features
- Perfect for small project teams or working groups

<Tip>
When a group message needs more structure (categories, bookmarks, etc.), convert it to a private channel.
</Tip>

## Channel Management

### Creating Channels

Create new channels to organize conversations:

1. Click the **+** icon next to "Channels" in the sidebar
2. Choose **Create New Channel**
3. Select Public or Private
4. Set channel details:
   - **Name** (1-64 characters, lowercase, no spaces)
   - **Display Name** (up to 64 characters)
   - **Purpose** (up to 250 characters)
   - **Header** (up to 1,024 characters)

**Channel Naming Rules**:
- 1-64 characters
- Lowercase letters, numbers, dashes, and underscores
- Must start with a letter
- No spaces (use dashes instead)

### Channel Header and Purpose

**Channel Purpose**:
- Brief description (max 250 characters)
- Helps users understand the channel's focus
- Displayed in channel browser

**Channel Header**:
- Detailed information (max 1,024 characters)
- Displayed at the top of the channel
- Supports Markdown formatting
- Perfect for links, guidelines, or important information

Example header:
```markdown
**Project Alpha** - Launch Q2 2024
📋 [Project Board](https://example.com/board) | 📊 [Metrics](https://example.com/metrics)
Daily standup at 9am ET | Questions? Ask @projectlead
```

### Channel Bookmarks

Pin important resources for quick access:
- Add links to documents, tools, or websites
- Upload files directly as bookmarks
- Organize with custom names and emojis
- Displayed prominently at the top of the channel
- Reorder bookmarks via drag-and-drop

<Note>
Channel bookmarks require Mattermost v7.0 or later and appropriate channel permissions.
</Note>

### Channel Categories

Organize your channels into custom categories:
- Create unlimited custom categories
- Drag channels between categories
- Collapse/expand categories
- Mute entire categories
- Default categories: Favorites, Channels, Direct Messages

Example organization:
```
⭐ Favorites
  - #project-alpha
  - #engineering-team

📁 Projects
  - #project-alpha
  - #project-beta
  - #marketing-campaign

🏢 Departments
  - #engineering
  - #marketing
  - #sales
```

## Channel Permissions

Control what users can do in channels:

### Member Roles

**Channel Admins**:
- Manage channel settings
- Add/remove members
- Delete channel
- Manage channel permissions

**Channel Members**:
- Post messages (if permitted)
- Read channel history
- React to messages
- Use channel features

**Channel Guests**:
- Limited to specific channels
- Cannot discover other channels
- Restricted permissions

### Advanced Permissions

Fine-grained control over channel actions:
- Who can post messages
- Who can create threads
- Who can upload files
- Who can add reactions
- Who can manage channel members
- Who can delete posts

<Tip>
Use permission schemes to apply consistent permissions across multiple channels.
</Tip>

## Channel Features

### Channel Browser

Discover and join public channels:
- Browse all public channels on your team
- Search by name or purpose
- See member count and channel activity
- Filter by archived status
- Join with a single click

### Channel Notifications

Customize notifications per channel:

**Notification Levels**:
- **All messages** - Notify for every message
- **Mentions only** - Only @mentions and keywords
- **Nothing** - No notifications (still see unread badge)

**Additional Options**:
- Mute channel completely
- Ignore @channel and @all mentions
- Custom notification sound
- Desktop, mobile, and email preferences

### Muting Channels

Mute channels to reduce noise:
- Muted channels appear dimmed in sidebar
- No desktop or mobile notifications
- Still see unread indicators
- Perfect for high-traffic channels you want to check periodically

### Favoriting Channels

Pin important channels to the Favorites category:
- Quick access to key channels
- Always visible at the top of your sidebar
- Star icon in channel name
- Stays favorited across devices

## Channel Sidebar

The channel sidebar provides quick navigation:

**Features**:
- **Unread indicator** - Bold channel names with unread counts
- **Mention badge** - Red badge showing mention count
- **Channel icons** - Visual indicators for channel types
- **Quick switcher** (Cmd/Ctrl + K) - Jump to any channel
- **Channel search** - Filter channels by name

**Status Indicators**:
- **Bold** - Unread messages
- **Red badge** - Mentions or DMs
- **Dimmed** - Muted channels
- **Star** - Favorited channels

## Shared Channels

Collaborate with external organizations:
- Share specific channels with other Mattermost installations
- Synchronized messages and files
- Secure, end-to-end communication
- Control exactly what is shared
- Support for public and private shared channels

<Warning>
Shared channels require configuration by system administrators and appropriate licensing.
</Warning>

## Channel Archiving

### Archiving Channels

Archive inactive channels to declutter:
- Archived channels are hidden from sidebar
- Channel history is preserved and searchable
- Can be unarchived if needed
- Links to archived channels still work
- No new messages can be posted

### Unarchiving Channels

Restore archived channels:
- Channel Admins or System Admins can unarchive
- All messages and history are restored
- Members can post again
- Channel reappears in sidebar for members

## Channel Limits

**Configuration Limits**:
- Channel name: 1-64 characters
- Display name: Up to 64 characters
- Header: Up to 1,024 characters
- Purpose: Up to 250 characters
- Channels per team: Configurable (default varies)
- Group message participants: 3-8 users

<Note>
System administrators can adjust these limits based on your organization's needs.
</Note>

## Best Practices

### Naming Conventions

Establish clear naming patterns:
```
#proj-[name]        - Project channels
#team-[name]        - Team channels  
#dept-[name]        - Department channels
#temp-[topic]       - Temporary channels
#[location]-office  - Location-based channels
```

### Channel Organization

1. **Use public channels by default** - Increase transparency
2. **Create focused channels** - One topic per channel
3. **Archive inactive channels** - Keep sidebar clean
4. **Set clear headers** - Help users understand the purpose
5. **Use categories** - Organize related channels together
6. **Add bookmarks** - Quick access to important resources

### Channel Lifecycle

1. **Creation** - Clear purpose and naming
2. **Active use** - Regular updates and engagement
3. **Maintenance** - Update header, add bookmarks
4. **Archiving** - When purpose is fulfilled or channel inactive
